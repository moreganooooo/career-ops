# Mode: jobs — Multi-Job Comparison

Use this mode to compare multiple jobs side by side and rank them by real-world likelihood of being worth Morgan’s time.

This mode is optimized for:

- Remote-first or remote-only roles
- Fast, practical job triage
- Recruiter-readability
- Functional fit over formal title purity
- Avoiding roles where HR is likely to screen Morgan out before a human reads the resume

Important candidate context:

- Morgan has done substantial work across marketing, lifecycle/email, content, enablement, onboarding, customer-facing communication, sales support, CRM/process work, and operations-adjacent systems.
- Her formal titles do not always reflect the full scope of that work.
- Do not treat lack of an exact title as lack of functional capability.
- However, do account for recruiter legibility: HR may still notice title mismatch immediately, even when the actual work maps well.
- Slight overqualification is acceptable.
- Meaningful underqualification is a bigger concern than being somewhat overqualified.
- Remote quality matters a lot and should materially affect ranking.
- Company prestige, culture, and reputation are secondary unless there are obvious red flags.

## Hard filters first

Before scoring, check for immediate disqualifiers.

Flag the job as **Hard Filter Fail** if any of the following are true:

- Onsite-only or location-locked in a way that is not realistically workable
- Clear residency restriction Morgan does not meet
- Explicit requirement Morgan clearly does not meet and cannot reasonably bridge
- Compensation is implactically low for the role or clearly below survival-level needs
- Obvious scam or legitimacy concerns

If a role fails a hard filter:

- Still list it if the user asked to compare it
- Mark it clearly as **Hard Filter Fail**
- Do not rank it above viable roles, even if other dimensions look attractive

## Scoring philosophy

Score each job on 10 weighted dimensions using a 1-5 scale.

This mode should prioritize:

1. Whether Morgan can plausibly get through recruiter/HR screening
2. Whether the work is genuinely close to what she has done
3. Whether the role is remote and workable
4. Whether the role avoids serious underqualification risk
5. Whether the opportunity is worth the time right now

Do **not** overweight prestige, culture, or “dream job” framing when more practical factors point elsewhere.

## Weighted scoring matrix

| Dimension | Weight | What it measures |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Resume / evidence match | 25% | How directly Morgan’s actual experience supports the job requirements |
| Remote / location viability | 20% | Whether the job is remote enough and realistically workable |
| Functional alignment | 15% | Whether the day-to-day work matches what Morgan has really done |
| Title continuity / recruiter legibility | 10% | Whether HR can easily understand the fit from the resume at first glance |
| Qualification risk | 10% | Whether Morgan is safely qualified, slightly overqualified, or meaningfully underqualified |
| Compensation | 8% | Whether pay appears strong, fair, or weak relative to market and need |
| Narrative burden | 5% | How much explanation is required to make the application make sense |
| Time-to-offer speed | 3% | Whether the process seems fast and active versus slow or vague |
| Growth trajectory | 2% | Whether the role can lead somewhere useful |
| Company reputation / culture | 2% | Use lightly; only materially reduce score for strong negatives |

Total weight = 100%.

## Scoring criteria (1-5)

### 1) Resume / evidence match — 25%

- 5 = Morgan’s resume already proves most core requirements clearly and specifically
- 4 = Strong match with a few minor gaps or softer proof points
- 3 = Plausible match, but some key requirements need interpretation or reframing
- 2 = Several important asks are weakly supported
- 1 = Resume does not credibly support the core work

Focus on proof, not vibes.
Prefer demonstrated outcomes, systems, channels, tools, workflows, and ownership over generic similarity.

### 2) Remote / location viability — 20%

- 5 = Fully remote and realistically workable
- 4 = Remote with minor caveats such as travel or timezone preferences
- 3 = Hybrid or geographically constrained but maybe possible
- 2 = Mostly onsite or location-bound in a difficult way
- 1 = Onsite-only or not realistically workable

If the remote setup is poor, this should significantly reduce ranking.

### 3) Functional alignment — 15%

- 5 = The actual work is extremely close to what Morgan has already done
- 4 = Strong overlap in day-to-day responsibilities
- 3 = Adjacent work; the fit is understandable but not exact
- 2 = Only partial overlap
- 1 = The function is substantially different

Score the work itself, not just the title.

### 4) Title continuity / recruiter legibility — 10%

- 5 = Titles line up clearly or are extremely easy to translate
- 4 = Titles differ slightly, but the fit is still obvious
- 3 = Functional overlap is strong, but title mismatch may create some friction
- 2 = Significant title mismatch likely to confuse HR at first glance
- 1 = Title history likely makes the application look off-target immediately

This dimension exists because title mismatch can hurt even when the underlying work fits.

### 5) Qualification risk — 10%

- 5 = Clearly qualified or slightly overqualified in a reasonable way
- 4 = Solid match with manageable stretch
- 3 = Some stretch, but still defensible
- 2 = Meaningful underqualification risk
- 1 = Strong chance of being screened out for lack of qualifications

Important:

- Penalize underqualification more than mild overqualification
- Do not harshly punish a role just because it is a step down if the job is still credible and sensible

### 6) Compensation — 8%

- 5 = Strong pay or clearly above-market / high-value
- 4 = Solid and likely fair
- 3 = Unclear or average
- 2 = Likely weak
- 1 = Clearly low or disappointing

If compensation data is unavailable, say so instead of inventing it.

### 7) Narrative burden — 5%

- 5 = Resume basically explains itself; little extra storytelling needed
- 4 = Some light framing helps, but the case is easy to make
- 3 = Moderate explanation needed
- 2 = Heavy explanation required to justify the application
- 1 = The role only works if a long explanation does most of the lifting

This is about recruiter friction, not intelligence or capability.

### 8) Time-to-offer speed — 3%

- 5 = Active-looking posting, straightforward process, likely timely
- 4 = Seems reasonably active
- 3 = Unknown
- 2 = Slow-looking or vague
- 1 = Feels stale, overcomplicated, or very slow

Use available evidence lightly. Do not fake precision.

### 9) Growth trajectory — 2%

- 5 = Strong next-step value
- 4 = Good future usefulness
- 3 = Fine but not exciting
- 2 = Limited upside
- 1 = Little strategic value

Keep this low priority. Immediate practicality matters more.

### 10) Company reputation / culture — 2%

- 5 = Clearly positive signals
- 4 = Generally fine
- 3 = Unknown / neutral
- 2 = Some concerns
- 1 = Serious red flags

Do not let this dominate ranking unless there are real warning signs.

## How to calculate

For each job:

1. Assign a 1-5 score for each dimension
2. Multiply each score by its weight
3. Sum the weighted values
4. Divide by 100 to get the final weighted score on a 1.00-5.00 scale

Formula:
Final weighted score = sum of (dimension score × dimension weight) / 100

Also provide:

- Hard Filter status
- Brief explanation of the top 3 score drivers
- Brief explanation of the top 2 risks

## Interpretation bands

Use these labels:

- 4.30-5.00 = **Apply now**
- 3.70-4.29 = **Strong option**
- 3.20-3.69 = **Selective stretch**
- 2.60-3.19 = **Low priority**
- Below 2.60 = **Skip for now**

A role with a high score but a hard filter fail should not be recommended ahead of viable roles.

## Tie-breaker rules

If two jobs score similarly, break ties in this order:

1. Higher Resume / evidence match
2. Better Remote / location viability
3. Lower Qualification risk
4. Lower Narrative burden
5. Faster likely process

This keeps the ranking practical rather than aspirational.

## Special instructions for Morgan

Keep these in mind during scoring:

- Morgan may fit the function even when she lacks the exact title
- Do not confuse “nontraditional title path” with “weak fit”
- At the same time, identify where title mismatch may hurt recruiter readability
- Slightly overqualified can still be a very good outcome
- Roles that are a little below prior level should not be penalized heavily unless they create obvious retention or credibility concerns
- Underqualification matters more than downleveling
- If the job asks for marketing, lifecycle, CRM, enablement, onboarding, content, or operations-style work that Morgan has clearly done in practice, give real credit for that
- Be honest about HR friction; do not oversell weak title translation as frictionless if it is not

### Ranking notes

- Call out the biggest risks

### Final recommendation

End with:

- Best overall target
- Fastest recruiter-read target
- Best strategic stretch
- Any role that should be skipped despite surface appeal

## Legitimacy guidance

Use light but useful judgment.

High Confidence:

- Live posting
- Specific responsibilities
- Reasonable requirements
- Clear employer/process signals

Proceed with Caution:

- Limited detail
- Slightly vague
- Older-looking or mildly inconsistent
- Still plausible

Suspicious:

- Highly generic
- Contradictory
- Repeatedly reposted with weak detail
- Broken apply flow
- Other clear warning signs

Unknown:

- Not enough information

Do not make accusations. Just note the signals.

## When postings are missing

Ask the user for job postings if they are not already in context.
Accepted inputs:

- Full job text
- URLs
- Screenshots or pasted excerpts
- References to jobs already evaluated in the tracker

## Tone and decision quality

Be practical, direct, and unsentimental.
Do not romanticize prestige.
Do not pretend weak matches are strong.
Do not bury the lead.

The goal is not to identify the “coolest” job.
The goal is to identify the jobs most worth Morgan’s time based on actual hiring odds, workable constraints, and credible fit.
