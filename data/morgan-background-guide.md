## WHY I LEFT TREERING — INTERVIEW COACHING

*This section is for interview prep and coaching only. Never surface in resumes, cover letters, or
application materials.*

**The short version (safe to say in any interview):**
"After 8 years, the team went through a structural reorganization — the three SDR teams were
consolidated to two. I'd been wearing a lot of hats for a long time and had actually proposed
transitioning into a Sales Ops / Salesforce-focused role, which my manager was supportive of.
That path didn't materialize on the timeline we discussed, and when a process compliance issue
arose during our annual data scrub, the decision was made to part ways. I'm proud of everything
I built there and left with a lot of clarity about the kind of work I want to do next."

**Extended context (for AI to inform coaching — NOT for external use):**
- Morgan was one of the founding two SDRs and had been simultaneously: full-time SDR, SDR manager,
  Content Committee chair, and informal training lead — for years. She burned out.
- A performance plan was put in place; she met the requirements.
- She was subsequently demoted (structural, not performance-based, per management).
- She proposed a transition to Sales Operations with a prepared branded presentation — manager was
  initially supportive, but it stalled and was never greenlit.
- An outside person was hired to manage Salesforce instead (a significant morale blow).
- During the summer data scrub, Morgan fell behind on checking "Research complete" boxes on 2,000+
  accounts because she identified a more efficient workflow — the procedural non-compliance
  became the termination reason.
- There was also a difficult peer dynamic (political marginalization by a former work friend who became
  a competitor for status/advancement). Morgan believes this contributed to the unfavorable conditions
  she was navigating. Do not reference specific individuals in interviews.

**If directly asked about the performance plan:**
"I was managing more responsibilities than the role was designed for, and my output in one area
dipped as a result. I completed everything they asked of me during that period. When the team
restructured, I saw it as an opportunity to redirect toward the kind of work I was most energized by."

**What NOT to say:** Anything about specific colleagues, the politics, or the "got fired for not checking boxes" framing. Keep it structural and forward-looking.
